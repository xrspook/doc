The Gandee Family of 'The Cerebral Palsy Swagger' visits Raw

The Gandee Family brought their message of Cerebral Palsy awareness to Raw in Cleveland! Hunter Gandee, 14, carried his brother, Braden Gandee, 7, for 40 miles in Michigan to raise awareness about kids with Cerebral Palsy.

The Gandee Family were special guests of WWE at the Quicken Loans Arena, where they met Superstars, Divas and WWE Chief Brand Officer Stephanie McMahon before Raw.

Hunter is a wrestler and football player at Bedford Junior High School. Braden, who has Cerebral Palsy, loves wrestling and is a big supporter of his older brother. Their cause is called "The Cerebral Palsy Swagger."

Hunter recently walked with 52-pound Braden on his back from the Bedford Junior High wrestling room to the University of Michigan wrestling room. The 40-mile walk took two days and Hunter wore a sling on his back to help support his brother’s weight.

Now the Gandees are raising money for Douglas Road Elementary School in Bedford, Mich., to transform the playground into a handicapped accessible playground that Braden can play on with his friends.
