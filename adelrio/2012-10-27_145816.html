12-Man Tag Team Match

Superstars vote "no confidence" against Triple H

Del Rio and some of Raw's Superstars are the next to leave.

WWE Champion Alberto Del Rio is one of many Raw Superstars ready to stand up to WWE COO Triple H.

Divas Champion Beth Phoenix leads her division out to the ring.

World Heavyweight Champion Mark Henry brings in a group from SmackDown.

WWE Tag Team Champions Air Boom are at the front of another group of Raw Superstars.

Even WWE Hall of Famer Jerry "The King" Lawler wants to vote.

Will Triple H receive a vote of confidence from the Superstars, Divas and crew?

The COO says everyone at the ring has the opportunity to tell him how they think he's doing running Raw.

Triple H is curious to hear their opinions.

Wade Barrett is selected by the group to say a few words about their "legitimate grievances."

The Game questions if Barrett has a problem with the chaos on Raw because he's not the one causing it?

Mike Chioda speaks for the group of WWE officials.

He tells the COO that for the first time in 23 years with WWE, he's afraid to come to work.

The Glamazon says the Divas are worried something bad - accidental or intentional - could happen to them.

Lawler says he's not speaking for anyone but himself, and he thinks Raw is "spiraling out of control."

"You're in charge and you're the reason all of this is happening," Lawler tells Triple H.

The WWE Hall of Famer reluctantly walks out of Raw SuperShow.

Christian calls for the vote, and it's a collective one of "no confidence."

John Lauranaitis, WWE's EVP of Talent Relations, emerges, shaking his head disapprovingly at the COO.

After all of the Superstars, Divas and crew leave, Triple H stands alone in the ring at the end of Raw SuperShow.
